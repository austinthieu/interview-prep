# Tell me about a time when you had a hard time working with someone on your team. How did you handle it?

**Situation:** During a software engineering course at university, I worked on a team project to develop a web application. Midway through the project, one teammate preferred to work independently and would make significant UI changes without discussing them with the rest of the team. This led to inconsistent designs and occasional merge conflicts when integrating our work.

**Task:** I wanted to keep the project on schedule while improving collaboration so that everyone was working toward the same design and functionality.

**Action:** I spoke with my teammate privately to better understand their perspective. They explained they were trying to move quickly and didn't realize the changes were affecting the rest of the team. We agreed to communicate design changes before implementing them and started holding short check-in meetings every few days to review progress and discuss upcoming work. We also used our Git repository more effectively by reviewing each other's changes before merging them.

**Result:** Our communication improved significantly, and we were able to complete the project on time with a consistent user interface. The experience taught me that addressing issues early through respectful communication is much more effective than letting frustration build. It also reinforced the importance of collaboration, especially when multiple developers are working on the same codebase.
