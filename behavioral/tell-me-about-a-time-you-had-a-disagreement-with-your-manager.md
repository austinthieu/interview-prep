# Tell me about a time you had a disagreement with your manager

**Situation:** While working at CNRL, I was supporting document and data management using Documentum for engineering and accounting teams. During one of the workflows, there was a request to quickly update and migrate a large batch of documents to meet an internal deadline. My supervisor preferred a faster manual update process to ensure everything was completed on time.

**Task:** My responsibility was to complete the updates accurately while minimizing disruption to the existing document structure and ensuring compliance with internal data management standards. I also needed to make sure the work was completed within the required timeline.

**Action:** After reviewing the workflow, I had concerns that doing everything manually under time pressure could lead to inconsistent metadata and potential classification errors in Documentum. I brought this up with my supervisor in a one-on-one discussion and acknowledged the urgency of the deadline. I suggested a more controlled approach where we could batch-process part of the updates and manually verify only the exceptions. I also offered to help test the approach on a small subset first to reduce risk.

**Result:** My supervisor agreed to adjust the process, and we used a hybrid approach combining batch updates with manual validation for edge cases. This reduced the risk of data inconsistencies while still allowing us to meet the deadline. It also improved the overall workflow for similar future document updates and reinforced the importance of balancing speed with data integrity in enterprise systems.
