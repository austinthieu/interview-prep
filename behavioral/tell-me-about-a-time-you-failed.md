### Tell me about a time you failed. How did you deal with the situation?

**Situation:** During a software development project, I was responsible for implementing a feature that involved updating and displaying data through a web interface. The feature had a tight deadline, and I was focused on delivering it on time for the next milestone.

**Task:** My responsibility was to ensure the feature worked correctly across different use cases and integrated properly with the existing backend and database without introducing issues into the application.

**Action:** In order to meet the deadline, I focused heavily on getting the feature functional and did not spend enough time testing edge cases, particularly around invalid or unexpected input. After deployment to the test environment, I discovered that the feature behaved incorrectly under certain conditions and caused inconsistent data to be displayed. I immediately took responsibility, investigated the issue, and traced it back to missing validation and insufficient testing coverage. I fixed the logic, added proper input validation, and expanded my testing to include edge cases. I also made sure to review similar parts of the codebase to prevent similar issues elsewhere.

**Result:** The issue was resolved quickly, and the updated version of the feature worked as expected across all test scenarios. While it caused a delay in that specific milestone, it helped improve the overall stability of the project. From this experience, I learned the importance of balancing speed with thorough testing and making validation and edge-case handling a standard part of my development process rather than an afterthought.
